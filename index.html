What Changes

Key differences between current law and the approved revision.[6] [7]

Residency for Naturalization
5 years
10 years (7 for EU/CPLP)[1]
Residency Clock Starts
From application date
From first residence card issuance[8]
Ancestry-Based Naturalization
Broader access
Limited to great-grandchildren[6]
Integration Requirements
Language knowledge
Language + culture + democratic values test + solemn declaration[6]
Children of Foreigners Born in PT
Parents with legal residence
Parents must have 3 years legal residence[6]
Sephardic Jewish Regime
Special naturalization pathway
Eliminated[6]
Spouse of Portuguese Citizen
Essentially unchanged[7]
3-year marriage rule remains. 6-year rule (no MP opposition) maintained. Civil partnerships now require judicial recognition.
Criminal Bars
Existed
Reinforced — 3+ year sentences bar naturalization[6]
No transitional protections. The approved law includes no grandfathering clause.[9] Applications filed before the law takes effect will be assessed under old rules (per TC 1133/2025 precedent[10]), but new applications filed after enforcement will face the new requirements.
