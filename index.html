Two separate decrees. Parliament approved two decrees: (1) the Nationality Law revision (residency requirements, etc.) and (2) a Penal Code amendment (loss of nationality as criminal penalty). PS says the nationality law's constitutional issues are "resolved" and is only evaluating a TC challenge on the Penal Code decree. The nationality law can proceed independently.

What Changes

Residency for Naturalization
Old: 5 years
New: 10 years (7 for EU/CPLP)
Residency Clock Starts
Old: from application date
New: from first residence card issuance
Ancestry-Based Naturalization
Old: broader access
New: limited to great-grandchildren
Integration Requirements
Old: language knowledge
New: language + culture + democratic values test + solemn declaration
Children of Foreigners Born in PT
Old: parents with legal residence
New: parents must have 3 years legal residence
Sephardic Jewish Regime
Old: special naturalization pathway
New: eliminated
Spouse of Portuguese Citizen
Essentially unchanged
3-year marriage rule remains. 6-year rule (no MP opposition) maintained. Civil partnerships now require judicial recognition.
Criminal Bars
Old: existed
New: reinforced — 3+ year sentences bar naturalization
No transitional protections. The approved law includes no grandfathering clause. Applications filed before the law takes effect will be assessed under the old rules (per TC 1133/2025 precedent), but new applications filed after enforcement will face the new requirements.
