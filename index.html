Post-Promulgation Timeline

What happens between Seguro's signature and the law taking effect. Critical: Art. 8 Decreto 48/XVII fixes in-force as the calendar day AFTER DR publication — no 5-day vacatio legis.[DEC48] The entire post-promulgation chain is compressed to ~2 days in the typical case.

Offset Step Actor / source

D0–D+1 Decree transmitted to INCM Presidential services · administrative handoff
D+1 (biz day) Publicação em DR, 1.ª série Imprensa Nacional · weekdays only; same-day possible for urgent items
D+2 (calendar) LAW IN FORCE Art. 8 Decreto 48/XVII: "dia seguinte à publicação"

Why prior "3-9 day" estimates were wrong

  • The 5-day default vacatio legis (Lei 74/98 art. 2.º n.º 2) does NOT apply — Art. 8 of this decree fixes its own in-force rule ("dia seguinte").
  • INCM delay for high-profile organic laws is 1 business day, not 3–6. Political-spotlight reform laws go to DR next business day as standard practice.
  • Referenda (PM counter-signature, Art. 140 CRP) happens same-day with promulgação — not a separate lag step.

Weekend / holiday modifiers

  • DR publishes Mon–Sat — primary: weekdays; Saturday rare, for urgent items. Never Sundays.
  • Upcoming closures: Sat 25 Apr (Dia da Liberdade), Fri 1 May (Dia do Trabalhador). A Thu/Fri promulgação straddling these dates pushes DR publication to the next working day.
  • "Dia seguinte" counts calendar days, including weekends. DR publishes Friday → law in force Saturday (administratively treated as effective from the Saturday date regardless).

Rev 25, 2 May 2026. Detailed modelling of S7 (24% probability) including the override arithmetic. Critical correction from earlier revisions: the nationality law is a lei orgânica (Art. 164 al. f + Art. 166 n.º 2 CRP), so the veto-override threshold is 2/3 of deputados presentes per Art. 136 n.º 3 CRP — not the simpler "absolute majority of 116" that applies to ordinary laws.

Constitutional procedure if Seguro vetoes (Art. 136 CRP):
  1. Veto message: Seguro returns the decree to the Assembly of the Republic with written reasons. Most likely date: Sun 3 May 2026 (deadline day).
