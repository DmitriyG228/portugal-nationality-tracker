# Legal Brief: Mandatory Admission of Free-Form Requerimento by Conservatória dos Registos Centrais

**Re:** Application for Portuguese nationality by naturalization under Art. 6.º, n.º 1 da Lei n.º 37/81, de 3 de outubro

**Date:** April 2026

---

## Purpose

This brief demonstrates that Portuguese law requires the Conservatória dos Registos Centrais (CRC) to admit a free-form requerimento that satisfies all elements of Art. 18.º n.º 4 of Decreto-Lei n.º 237-A/2006, and that neither the use of a specific form (Modelo 6.1) nor a declaration of "at least five years" of residency is a legal precondition for admission. It further demonstrates that an application filed under the current law remains pending and is assessed under current rules, even if a new law enters into force during the proceedings.

---

## Question Presented

Must the Conservatória dos Registos Centrais admit a nationality application submitted as a free-form requerimento -- rather than on the IRN Modelo 6.1 template -- when the requerimento contains all elements mandated by Art. 18.º n.º 4 of DL 237-A/2006 and is accompanied by all required documents?

**Short answer:** Yes. The law defines the required content of the requerimento (Art. 18.º n.º 4) and the exclusive grounds for summary rejection (Art. 27.º n.º 2). Neither provision requires a specific form. The Modelo 6.1 is a "Minuta" (template), not a "formulario obrigatorio" (mandatory form). No portaria or other legal instrument mandates its use.

---

## I. The Legal Chain -- Seven Propositions

### Proposition 1: The law defines exactly what the requerimento must contain

Art. 18.º n.º 4 of DL 237-A/2006 enumerates every element the requerimento must include. The list is exhaustive.

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «O requerimento e redigido em lingua portuguesa e, alem do fundamento do pedido e de outras circunstancias que o interessado considere relevantes, deve conter os seguintes elementos: a) O nome completo, data do nascimento, sexo, estado civil, naturalidade, nacionalidade, filiacao, profissao e residencia atual do interessado, bem como a indicacao dos paises onde tenha residido anteriormente; b) O nome completo e residencia dos representantes legais, caso o interessado seja menor ou maior acompanhado que careca de representacao para o ato, ou do procurador; c) A mencao do numero, data e entidade emitente do titulo ou autorizacao de residencia, passaporte ou documento de identificacao equivalente do interessado, bem como do representante legal ou do procurador, se os houver; d) A assinatura do interessado, dos representantes legais ou do procurador.»

> **Translation:**
> "The application is written in Portuguese and, besides the legal basis of the request and other circumstances the applicant considers relevant, must contain the following elements: a) Full name, date of birth, sex, marital status, place of birth, nationality, parentage, profession, and current residence of the applicant, as well as indication of countries of previous residence; b) Full name and residence of legal representatives, if the applicant is a minor or an accompanied adult requiring representation, or of the proxy; c) The number, date, and issuing entity of the residence title or authorization, passport, or equivalent identification document of the applicant, as well as of the legal representative or proxy, if any; d) The signature of the applicant, legal representatives, or proxy."

**Source:** [Art. 18.º DL 237-A/2006 (pgdlisboa.pt, consolidated)](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=895A0018&nid=895&tabela=leis)

**Therefore:** The law requires personal identification data, residence document details, and a signature. It does NOT require any declaration about the duration of residency. It does NOT reference any specific form, modelo, or template. A requerimento containing all of the above elements satisfies Art. 18.º n.º 4 regardless of format.

---

### Proposition 2: CRC can only reject the requerimento for missing Art. 18.º n.º 4 elements

Art. 27.º n.º 2 of DL 237-A/2006 defines the exclusive grounds for summary rejection (indeferimento liminar). Ground (a) concerns the requerimento itself:

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «No prazo de 30 dias contados a partir da data da rececao, a Conservatoria dos Registos Centrais deve analisar sumariamente o processo e proceder ao indeferimento liminar do requerimento nos seguintes casos: a) Quando nao contenha os elementos previstos no n.º 4 do artigo 18.º;»

> **Translation:**
> "Within 30 days from receipt, the Conservatoria dos Registos Centrais must summarily review the application and proceed with summary rejection in the following cases: a) When it does not contain the elements provided for in paragraph 4 of article 18;"

**Source:** [Art. 27.º DL 237-A/2006 (pgdlisboa.pt, consolidated)](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=895A0027&nid=895&tabela=leis)

**Therefore:** The sole ground for rejecting the requerimento itself is the absence of Art. 18.º n.º 4 elements. If all elements enumerated in Proposition 1 are present, rejection under Art. 27.º n.º 2(a) is legally impossible -- regardless of whether the applicant used Modelo 6.1 or a free-form letter.

---

### Proposition 3: Missing documents that are exempted by Art. 37.º cannot be grounds for rejection

Art. 27.º n.º 2(b) provides a second ground for summary rejection -- missing documents -- but explicitly carves out exemptions:

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «b) Quando nao seja acompanhado dos documentos necessarios para comprovar os factos que constituem o fundamento do pedido, sem prejuizo do disposto nos n.os 4 a 8 do artigo 37.º;»

> **Translation:**
> "b) When not accompanied by the documents necessary to prove the facts constituting the basis of the request, without prejudice to the provisions of paragraphs 4 to 8 of article 37;"

**Source:** Same as Proposition 2 ([Art. 27.º DL 237-A/2006](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=895A0027&nid=895&tabela=leis))

**Therefore:** The phrase «sem prejuizo do disposto nos n.os 4 a 8 do artigo 37.º» explicitly excludes from the rejection grounds any document that is exempted under Art. 37.º paragraphs 4 through 8. This carve-out is critical: documents that CRC obtains oficiosamente (Proposition 4) cannot serve as grounds for rejection.

---

### Proposition 4: AIMA residence proof and Portuguese criminal record are obtained by CRC -- not by the applicant

Art. 37.º n.º 7 of DL 237-A/2006 exempts two categories of documents:

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «Os interessados estao, igualmente, dispensados de apresentar os seguintes documentos, os quais sao oficiosamente obtidos junto das entidades competentes, sempre que possivel, por via eletronica: a) Certificado do registo criminal portugues; b) Documentos emitidos pela AIMA, I. P., destinados a comprovar a residencia legal em territorio portugues.»

> **Translation:**
> "Applicants are likewise exempted from submitting the following documents, which are officially obtained from the competent entities, whenever possible, by electronic means: a) Portuguese criminal record certificate; b) Documents issued by AIMA, I.P., intended to prove legal residence in Portuguese territory."

**Source:** [Art. 37.º DL 237-A/2006 (pgdlisboa.pt, consolidated)](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=895A0037&nid=895&tabela=leis)

**Therefore:** The AIMA residence certification and the Portuguese criminal record are obtained by CRC itself, not supplied by the applicant. Their absence from the application envelope cannot constitute a ground for rejection under Art. 27.º n.º 2(b), as confirmed by that provision's explicit carve-out for Art. 37.º n.os 4-8 (Proposition 3). The residence verification happens during the investigation phase (instruucao), not at intake.

---

### Proposition 5: Even if CRC intends to reject, the applicant must be notified and given 30 days to respond

Art. 27.º n.º 3 of DL 237-A/2006 establishes a mandatory procedural safeguard:

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «Se o conservador de registos ou o oficial de registos concluir que o requerimento deve ser liminarmente indeferido, o interessado e notificado dos fundamentos que conduzem ao indeferimento para que se pronuncie no prazo de 30 dias.»

> **Translation:**
> "If the registrar or registry official concludes that the application should be summarily rejected, the applicant is notified of the grounds for rejection so that they may respond within 30 days."

**Source:** Same as Proposition 2 ([Art. 27.º DL 237-A/2006](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=895A0027&nid=895&tabela=leis))

**Therefore:** No silent rejection is legally possible. Even in a hypothetical scenario where CRC questions the free-form format, the applicant receives written notice of the grounds and has 30 days to respond -- to supply missing elements, contest the grounds, or both. The filing date (postal stamp) is already registered at the time of this exchange.

---

### Proposition 6: Modelo 6.1 is a template ("Minuta"), not a mandatory form ("formulario obrigatorio")

Three independent pieces of evidence:

**(a) The form's own designation:**

The footer of the IRN Modelo 6.1 PDF reads:

> «Minuta 6.1 LN (Lei da Nacionalidade) - Processado por computador - irn.justica.gov.pt»

The term «Minuta» means template or draft. It is not «Formulario obrigatorio» (mandatory form).

**Source:** [Modelo 6.1 PDF (irn.justica.gov.pt)](https://irn.justica.gov.pt/Portals/33/Impressos/Nacionalidade/Modelo%20%206.1.pdf)

**(b) The IRN postal submission page uses non-mandatory language:**

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «Preencha o modelo de requerimento de Nacionalidade. Os modelos de requerimento estao disponiveis na pagina Impressos.»

> **Translation:**
> "Fill in the nationality application template. Application templates are available on the Forms page."

The term «modelo de requerimento» (application template) is guidance, not a legal mandate. The word «formulario obrigatorio» does not appear anywhere on this page.

**Source:** [justica.gov.pt/Servicos/Pedido-de-nacionalidade-por-correio](https://justica.gov.pt/Servicos/Pedido-de-nacionalidade-por-correio) (updated 03 Jul 2025)

**(c) No portaria or legal instrument mandates the form:**

Art. 18.º of DL 237-A/2006 requires a «requerimento» and defines its content in n.º 4. No provision of DL 237-A/2006, Lei 37/81, or any portaria establishes a specific form as the exclusive permissible format. The form's own warning acknowledges this distinction: «A falta de qualquer mencao, que, **por forca da lei**, deva constar do requerimento» -- "by force of law." Only what the law requires is mandatory.

**Therefore:** Modelo 6.1 is an administrative convenience, not a legal requirement. Art. 27.º n.º 2(a) tests for Art. 18.º n.º 4 elements, not for the use of a particular template.

---

### Proposition 7: The "5 years" declaration is not required by law -- and signing it before 5 years would be a criminal offense

**(a) It is not required:**

Art. 18.º n.º 4 (Proposition 1) lists every element the requerimento must contain. The declaration «tem residencia legal no territorio portugues ha pelo menos cinco anos» does not appear in the statute. It is added by the Modelo 6.1 template at Q2:

> **Modelo 6.1 Q2 (verbatim):**
> «declara que tem residencia legal no territorio portugues ha pelo menos cinco anos»

> **Translation:**
> "declares that he/she has legal residence in Portuguese territory for at least five years"

This declaration is a form addition, not a statutory requirement.

**(b) Signing it before 5 years would constitute a criminal offense:**

Art. 348.º-A n.os 1-2 of the Codigo Penal:

> **Portuguese (verbatim, n.º 1):**
> «Quem declarar ou atestar falsamente a autoridade publica ou a funcionario no exercicio das suas funcoes identidade, estado ou outra qualidade a que a lei atribua efeitos juridicos, proprios ou alheios, e punido com pena de prisao ate um ano ou com pena de multa, se pena mais grave nao lhe couber por forca de outra disposicao legal.»

> **Translation:**
> "Anyone who falsely declares or attests to a public authority or official in the exercise of their functions their identity, status, or other quality to which the law attributes legal effects, their own or others', is punished with imprisonment up to one year or a fine, unless a more serious penalty applies by force of another legal provision."

> **Portuguese (verbatim, n.º 2):**
> «Se as declaracoes se destinarem a ser exaradas em documento autentico o agente e punido com pena de prisao ate dois anos ou com pena de multa.»

> **Translation:**
> "If the declarations are intended to be recorded in an authentic document, the agent is punished with imprisonment up to two years or a fine."

**Source:** [Art. 348.º-A CP (pgdlisboa.pt, consolidated)](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=109A0348A&nid=109&tabela=leis)

Additionally, Art. 12.º-A of Lei 37/81:

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «E nulo o ato que determine a atribuicao, aquisicao ou perda da nacionalidade portuguesa com fundamento em documentos falsos ou certificativos de factos inveridicos ou inexistentes, ou ainda em falsas declaracoes.»

> **Translation:**
> "Any act determining the attribution, acquisition, or loss of Portuguese nationality based on false documents, certificates of untrue or nonexistent facts, or false declarations is null."

**Source:** [Art. 12.º-A Lei 37/81 (pgdlisboa.pt, consolidated)](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=614A0012A&nid=614&tabela=leis)

**Therefore:** The applicant uses a free-form requerimento not by preference but by legal necessity. Signing the Modelo 6.1 declaration before having 5 years of residency would constitute a false declaration under Art. 348.º-A CP (criminal penalty: up to 2 years imprisonment) and would render any resulting nationality grant null under Art. 12.º-A Lei 37/81. The free-form approach is not evasion -- it is compliance with criminal law.

## II. Why the Application Stays Pending

### A. Art. 6.º n.º 1 does not anchor the 5-year requirement to the filing date

> **Portuguese (verbatim, Art. 6.º n.º 1(b) Lei 37/81):**
> «O Governo concede a nacionalidade portuguesa, por naturalizacao, aos estrangeiros que satisfacam cumulativamente os seguintes requisitos: [...] b) Residirem legalmente no territorio portugues ha pelo menos cinco anos;»

> **Translation:**
> "The Government grants Portuguese nationality, by naturalization, to foreigners who cumulatively satisfy the following requirements: [...] b) Have legally resided in Portuguese territory for at least five years;"

**Source:** [Art. 6.º Lei 37/81 (pgdlisboa.pt, consolidated)](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=614A0006&nid=614&tabela=leis)

The provision says «residirem [...] ha pelo menos cinco anos» without any temporal anchor such as «a data do pedido» (at the date of application). This is not an omission -- contrast with Art. 6.º n.º 8 of the same law, which explicitly requires five years «imediatamente anteriores ao pedido» (immediately preceding the application). The legislature used precise temporal language where it intended a filing-date anchor and chose not to use it for Art. 6(1)(b).

### B. TC 1133/2025 confirms: requirements are assessed at the date of decision, not the date of filing

The Constitutional Court's Acordao n.º 1133/2025 struck down a legislative attempt to change this rule:

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «O decreto pretendia fazer depender o deferimento de pedidos pendentes do preenchimento de requisitos na data de apresentacao, e nao na data da decisao, como ocorre no regime em vigor.»

> **Translation:**
> "The decree intended to make the approval of pending applications depend on the fulfillment of requirements at the date of submission, and not at the date of decision, as occurs in the regime in force."

**Source:** [Acordao TC n.º 1133/2025](https://www.tribunalconstitucional.pt/tc/acordaos/20251133.html) (published DR I serie n.º 2/2026, 05.01.2026)

**Therefore:** Under the current regime -- which the TC confirmed as constitutionally required -- the 5-year residency requirement is assessed at the date of decision, not the date of filing. An application filed before the 5-year mark remains pending; residency continues to accrue while the application is processed.

### C. Residency is verified during instruucao, not at intake

After the intake review, if the application is not rejected, CRC initiates the investigation phase:

> **Portuguese (verbatim, Art. 27.º n.º 5 DL 237-A/2006):**
> «Nao ocorrendo indeferimento liminar, a Conservatoria dos Registos Centrais solicita as informacoes necessarias a Policia Judiciaria, a Unidade de Coordenacao de Fronteiras e Estrangeiros [...] e a AIMA, I. P.»

> **Translation:**
> "If summary rejection does not occur, the Conservatoria dos Registos Centrais requests the necessary information from the Judicial Police, the Border and Foreign Affairs Coordination Unit [...] and AIMA, I.P."

**Source:** Same as Proposition 2 ([Art. 27.º DL 237-A/2006](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=895A0027&nid=895&tabela=leis))

The actual residency duration is checked when CRC requests AIMA data during instruucao -- not at the intake stage. By the time CRC reaches the decision point, additional months of residence will have accrued.

### D. Filing before promulgation locks in the current law

The new nationality law (voted 1 April 2026) raises the residency requirement from 5 to 10 years. As of this writing, it has NOT been promulgated, NOT published in the Diario da Republica, and is NOT in force. The current Art. 6.º n.º 1(b) still requires «cinco anos».

Applications filed under the current law are assessed under the current law's rules. TC 1133/2025 protects pending applications from retroactive application of more restrictive requirements: the regime in force at the time of filing governs the assessment.

---

## III. Conclusion

The chain of reasoning is complete:

1. **Art. 18.º n.º 4 DL 237-A/2006** defines the requerimento's required content: personal identification data, residence document details, and signature. No specific form is mandated. No declaration about residency duration is required.

2. **Art. 27.º n.º 2(a) DL 237-A/2006** permits summary rejection ONLY when Art. 18.º n.º 4 elements are absent. All elements are present in the free-form requerimento.

3. **Art. 27.º n.º 2(b) DL 237-A/2006** permits rejection for missing documents, BUT explicitly carves out Art. 37.º n.os 4-8 exemptions.

4. **Art. 37.º n.º 7 DL 237-A/2006** exempts AIMA residence proof and Portuguese criminal record -- CRC obtains them oficiosamente.

5. **Art. 27.º n.º 3 DL 237-A/2006** guarantees 30 days' written notice before any rejection. No silent rejection is possible. The filing date is already registered.

6. **Modelo 6.1** is a «Minuta» (template). No law mandates its use. Art. 27.º n.º 2(a) tests for Art. 18.º n.º 4 elements, not for a particular form.

7. **The "5 years" declaration** is a Modelo 6.1 addition, absent from Art. 18.º n.º 4. Signing it before 5 years of residency would constitute a false declaration under **Art. 348.º-A CP** (up to 2 years imprisonment) and render any nationality grant null under **Art. 12.º-A Lei 37/81**.

8. **TC 1133/2025** confirms that under the current regime, substantive requirements are assessed at the date of decision, not the date of filing. The application remains pending while residency accrues.

**CRC is legally obligated to admit the free-form requerimento. There is no lawful basis for rejection.**
