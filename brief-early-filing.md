# Legal Brief: Early Filing and Assessment at Decision Date

**Re:** Application for Portuguese nationality by naturalization under Art. 6.º, n.º 1 da Lei n.º 37/81, de 3 de outubro -- filed before the 5-year residency mark is reached

**Date:** April 2026

---

## Purpose

This brief demonstrates that Portuguese law permits filing a nationality application under Art. 6.º n.º 1 of Lei 37/81 before the applicant has completed five years of legal residence, and that the substantive requirements -- including the residency duration -- are assessed at the date of decision, not the date of filing. It further demonstrates that the application cannot be rejected at intake for insufficient residency, that the new law (voted 1 April 2026) does not apply to applications filed before its promulgation, and that the Constitutional Court has confirmed this temporal framework.

---

## Question Presented

May a nationality application under Art. 6.º n.º 1 of Lei 37/81 be filed before the applicant has completed five years of legal residence, and must the Conservatoria dos Registos Centrais (CRC) assess the residency requirement at the date of decision rather than the date of filing?

**Short answer:** Yes. Art. 6.º n.º 1(b) does not anchor the five-year requirement to the date of filing. The Constitutional Court (Acordao TC 1133/2025) confirmed that under the current regime, requirements are assessed «na data da decisao» (at the date of decision). Residency is verified during the instruucao phase -- not at intake -- when CRC requests data from AIMA. The intake review (Art. 27.º n.º 2) tests only for the presence of Art. 18.º n.º 4 elements and required documents, not for whether substantive requirements are already met.

---

## I. Art. 6.º n.º 1 Does Not Anchor the 5-Year Requirement to the Filing Date

### Proposition 1: The statute requires five years of residence -- without specifying when

Art. 6.º n.º 1 of Lei 37/81 lists five cumulative requirements for naturalization:

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «O Governo concede a nacionalidade portuguesa, por naturalizacao, aos estrangeiros que satisfacam cumulativamente os seguintes requisitos: a) Serem maiores ou emancipados a face da lei portuguesa; b) Residirem legalmente no territorio portugues ha pelo menos cinco anos; c) Conhecerem suficientemente a lingua portuguesa; d) Nao tenham sido condenados, com transito em julgado da sentenca, com pena de prisao igual ou superior a 3 anos, por crime punivel segundo a lei portuguesa; e) Nao constituam perigo ou ameaca para a seguranca ou a defesa nacional, nomeadamente pelo envolvimento em atividades relacionadas com a pratica de terrorismo, criminalidade violenta, especialmente violenta ou altamente organizada.»

> **Translation:**
> "The Government grants Portuguese nationality, by naturalization, to foreigners who cumulatively satisfy the following requirements: a) Are of legal age or emancipated under Portuguese law; b) Have legally resided in Portuguese territory for at least five years; c) Have sufficient knowledge of the Portuguese language; d) Have not been convicted, with a final judgment, to imprisonment of 3 or more years for a crime punishable under Portuguese law; e) Do not constitute a danger or threat to national security, namely through involvement in activities related to the practice of terrorism, violent, especially violent, or highly organized crime."

**Source:** [Art. 6.º Lei 37/81 (pgdlisboa.pt, consolidated)](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=614A0006&nid=614&tabela=leis)

**Critical observation:** Alinea (b) says «residirem legalmente no territorio portugues ha pelo menos cinco anos» -- it does NOT say «a data do pedido» (at the date of application), «a data da apresentacao» (at the date of submission), or «imediatamente anteriores ao pedido» (immediately preceding the application).

---

### Proposition 2: This omission is deliberate -- contrast with Art. 6.º n.º 8 of the same law

Art. 6.º n.º 8 of Lei 37/81, which governs naturalization for ascendants of Portuguese citizens, uses explicit filing-date language:

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «O Governo pode conceder a nacionalidade, por naturalizacao, com dispensa do requisito estabelecido na alinea b) do n.º 1, aos individuos que sejam ascendentes de cidadaos portugueses originarios, aqui tenham residencia, independentemente de titulo, **ha pelo menos cinco anos imediatamente anteriores ao pedido** e desde que a ascendencia tenha sido estabelecida no momento do nascimento do cidadao portugues.»

> **Translation:**
> "The Government may grant nationality, by naturalization, with dispensation of the requirement in paragraph 1(b), to individuals who are ascendants of Portuguese citizens by birth, who have resided here, regardless of title, **for at least five years immediately preceding the application**, provided that the ascendancy was established at the moment of the Portuguese citizen's birth."

**Source:** Same as Proposition 1 ([Art. 6.º Lei 37/81](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=614A0006&nid=614&tabela=leis))

**Therefore:** The legislature knew how to anchor a residency requirement to the filing date -- it did so in Art. 6(8) with «imediatamente anteriores ao pedido». It chose NOT to use this language in Art. 6(1)(b). Under standard principles of statutory interpretation, this difference is meaningful: where the legislature uses precise temporal language in one provision and omits it in another within the same article, the omission is intentional.

| Provision | Temporal language | Filing-date anchor? |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Art. 6.º n.º 1(b) | «ha pelo menos cinco anos» | **None** |
| Art. 6.º n.º 8 | «ha pelo menos cinco anos **imediatamente anteriores ao pedido**» | **Explicit** |

## II. The Constitutional Court Confirmed: Assessment at Decision Date

### Background: What Parliament tried to do

In October 2025, Parliament approved Decreto n.º 17/XVII amending the Nationality Law. Art. 7.º of that decree contained transitional provisions (n.os 3 and 4) that would have changed when requirements are assessed for pending applications:

> **Art. 7.º n.º 3 of Decreto 17/XVII (the provision struck down):**
> The decree stipulated that the granting of pending nationality applications depends on fulfilling requirements «a data da sua apresentacao» (at the date of their submission) -- i.e., at the date the application was originally filed.

> **Art. 7.º n.º 4 of Decreto 17/XVII:**
> Declared n.º 3 to be interpretive in nature («natureza interpretativa»), which would have given it retroactive effect -- applying the filing-date rule even to applications already pending.

The Constitutional Court struck down both provisions unanimously.

---

### Proposition 3: TC 1133/2025 confirmed that the current regime assesses requirements at decision date -- not filing date

On 15 December 2025, the Plenary of the Constitutional Court (13 judges, unanimous on this point) ruled in Acordao n.º 1133/2025 (Processo n.º 1383/2025, Relatora: Conselheira Dora Lucas Neto).

The TC's official press release (Comunicado) describes the struck-down provision and contrasts it with the current regime:

> **Portuguese (verbatim, from TC Comunicado):**
> «A norma dos n.ºs 3 e 4 do artigo 7.º do Decreto, segundo a qual o deferimento dos pedidos de atribuicao ou aquisicao da nacionalidade pendentes a data da entrada em vigor das alteracoes a Lei da Nacionalidade depende do preenchimento dos requisitos previstos a data da apresentacao do pedido e nao, como sucede no regime atualmente em vigor, a data da decisao do pedido.»

> **Translation:**
> "The norm of paragraphs 3 and 4 of article 7 of the Decree, according to which the granting of applications for attribution or acquisition of nationality pending on the date of entry into force of the amendments to the Nationality Law depends on the fulfillment of the requirements provided for at the date of submission of the application and not, **as occurs in the regime currently in force, at the date of the decision on the application**."

**Source:** [TC Comunicado, Acordao n.º 1133/2025](https://www.tribunalconstitucional.pt/tc/imprensa0200-bd9183.html) | [Full ruling](https://www.tribunalconstitucional.pt/tc/acordaos/20251133.html) | Published DR I serie n.º 2/2026, 05.01.2026

**Five critical elements of this passage:**

1. **«pedidos [...] pendentes»** (pending applications) -- the Court was specifically addressing applications that have been filed and are awaiting decision. This is exactly the scenario of an application filed before the 5-year mark.

2. **«a data da decisao do pedido»** (at the date of the decision on the application) -- the Court identifies this as the moment when requirements are assessed under the current regime.

3. **«como sucede no regime atualmente em vigor»** (as occurs in the regime currently in force) -- this is not dicta, not hypothetical, not a policy preference. The Court is describing the existing, operative legal framework as a matter of constitutional fact.

4. **«a data da apresentacao do pedido»** (at the date of submission of the application) -- this is what Parliament tried to change to. The Court struck it down.

5. **Unanimity** -- all 13 judges of the Plenary agreed on this point. There were no dissenting opinions on the temporal assessment question.

---

### Proposition 3A: The TC found the filing-date rule unconstitutional because it violates the principle of protection of trust

The TC did not merely prefer decision-date assessment -- it held that changing to filing-date assessment for pending applications violates a constitutional principle:

> **Portuguese (verbatim, from TC Comunicado):**
> «O Tribunal Constitucional concluiu ocorrer violacao do principio da protecao da confianca, insito no principio do Estado de direito consagrado no artigo 2.º da Constituicao, por afrontar as legitimas expectativas dos destinatarios com procedimentos pendentes na aplicabilidade do regime existente na data da apresentacao do pedido.»

> **Translation:**
> "The Constitutional Court concluded that there is a violation of the principle of protection of trust, inherent in the rule of law principle enshrined in article 2 of the Constitution, by affronting the legitimate expectations of recipients with pending procedures in the applicability of the regime existing at the date of submission of the application."

**Source:** Same TC Comunicado as above.

**The TC's reasoning chain:**

1. **Constitutional anchor:** Art. 2.º of the Constitution establishes the principle of the rule of law («Estado de direito»).

2. **Derived principle:** The rule of law includes the principle of protection of trust («principio da protecao da confianca»).

3. **Legitimate expectations:** Applicants who filed nationality applications under the current regime had a legitimate expectation that the regime in force at filing -- including decision-date assessment -- would govern their applications.

4. **Violation:** Retroactively changing the assessment moment from decision date to filing date for pending applications «afronta» (affronts/violates) those legitimate expectations.

5. **Consequence:** The provision is unconstitutional under Art. 2.º CRP.

---

### Proposition 3B: The ruling also protected the residency start-date calculation for pending applications

The MLGTS legal analysis of TC 1133/2025 notes that the Court also addressed a related temporal issue -- the repeal of a favorable rule for calculating when the residency period begins:

> The Court «considered that the repeal of this rule would frustrate legitimate expectations and, as such, violate the principle of trust.»

The affected provision determined that «the period of residence begins from the moment of application for a temporary residence permit (and not from its issuance), provided that it is granted.»

**Source:** [MLGTS Legal Alert](https://www.mlgts.pt/en/knowledge/legal-alerts/Changes-to-the-law-of-nationality-constitutional-court-ruling-and-presidential-veto/26467/)

This reinforces the broader principle: the TC protects the temporal framework under which applicants filed. Changing the rules mid-process -- whether the assessment date or the residency calculation method -- violates the principle of trust for pending applications.

---

### Proposition 4: The TC ruling is binding, final, and cannot be overridden by administrative practice

Constitutional Court rulings in fiscalizacao preventiva are final:

> **Constitutional basis:** Art. 282.º n.º 1 CRP -- «A declaracao de inconstitucionalidade ou de ilegalidade com forca obrigatoria geral produz efeitos desde a entrada em vigor da norma declarada inconstitucional ou ilegal.»

> **Translation:** "The declaration of unconstitutionality or illegality with generally binding force takes effect from the entry into force of the norm declared unconstitutional or illegal."

Even if CRC's internal practice were to assess requirements at the filing date, this practice would be legally incorrect under TC 1133/2025. The applicant has a constitutional right to have requirements assessed at the decision date, as the TC confirmed is the operative rule.

**The new law (voted 1 April 2026)** is Parliament's response to TC 1133/2025 (a reapreciacao under Art. 279.º n.º 2 CRP). Parliament removed Art. 7.º n.os 3-4 in the revised text -- acknowledging it could not override the TC's ruling on the temporal question. But until the new law is promulgated and published in the Diario da Republica, it has no legal force. The TC's characterization of the current regime remains the authoritative statement of the law.

**What the April 2026 reapreciacao means for this argument:** Parliament's removal of the filing-date transitional provision from the revised decree is itself an acknowledgment that decision-date assessment is the constitutional baseline. The new law may change prospective rules (5 years to 10 years), but it cannot retroactively apply the filing-date assessment that the TC struck down.

## III. The Procedure Confirms: Residency Is Checked During Instruucao, Not at Intake

### Proposition 5: Intake review tests only for Art. 18.º n.º 4 elements and required documents -- not substantive requirements

Art. 27.º n.º 2 of DL 237-A/2006 defines the exclusive grounds for summary rejection at intake:

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «No prazo de 30 dias contados a partir da data da rececao, a Conservatoria dos Registos Centrais deve analisar sumariamente o processo e proceder ao indeferimento liminar do requerimento nos seguintes casos: a) Quando nao contenha os elementos previstos no n.º 4 do artigo 18.º; b) Quando nao seja acompanhado dos documentos necessarios para comprovar os factos que constituem o fundamento do pedido, sem prejuizo do disposto nos n.os 4 a 8 do artigo 37.º; c) Quando a apresentacao por via eletronica ao abrigo do n.º 2 do artigo 18.º nao se realize nos termos definidos na portaria a que se refere o n.º 1 do artigo 43.º-A.»

> **Translation:**
> "Within 30 days from receipt, the Conservatoria dos Registos Centrais must summarily review the application and proceed with summary rejection in the following cases: a) When it does not contain the elements provided for in paragraph 4 of article 18; b) When not accompanied by the documents necessary to prove the facts constituting the basis of the request, without prejudice to the provisions of paragraphs 4 to 8 of article 37; c) When electronic submission does not comply with the terms defined in the portaria referred to in paragraph 1 of article 43-A."

**Source:** [Art. 27.º DL 237-A/2006 (pgdlisboa.pt, consolidated)](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=895A0027&nid=895&tabela=leis)

**What is NOT in this list:** There is no ground for rejection based on the applicant not yet meeting the substantive requirements of Art. 6.º n.º 1 (residency duration, age, language, criminal record, security). The intake review is a formal check -- does the requerimento have the required elements? Are the documents enclosed? -- not a substantive assessment of whether the applicant already qualifies.

**Therefore:** "Residency not yet 5 years" is not an intake rejection ground. CRC cannot reject an application at the liminar stage because the applicant has not yet accumulated five years of residence.

---

### Proposition 6: AIMA residence data is obtained by CRC during instruucao -- after intake

Art. 37.º n.º 7 of DL 237-A/2006 exempts the applicant from providing AIMA residence documentation:

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «Os interessados estao, igualmente, dispensados de apresentar os seguintes documentos, os quais sao oficiosamente obtidos junto das entidades competentes, sempre que possivel, por via eletronica: a) Certificado do registo criminal portugues; b) Documentos emitidos pela AIMA, I. P., destinados a comprovar a residencia legal em territorio portugues.»

> **Translation:**
> "Applicants are likewise exempted from submitting the following documents, which are officially obtained from the competent entities, whenever possible, by electronic means: a) Portuguese criminal record certificate; b) Documents issued by AIMA, I.P., intended to prove legal residence in Portuguese territory."

**Source:** [Art. 37.º DL 237-A/2006 (pgdlisboa.pt, consolidated)](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=895A0037&nid=895&tabela=leis)

And Art. 27.º n.º 5 specifies when this data is obtained:

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «Nao ocorrendo indeferimento liminar, a Conservatoria dos Registos Centrais solicita as informacoes necessarias a Policia Judiciaria, a Unidade de Coordenacao de Fronteiras e Estrangeiros [...] e a AIMA, I. P.»

> **Translation:**
> "If summary rejection does not occur, the Conservatoria dos Registos Centrais requests the necessary information from the Judicial Police, the Border and Foreign Affairs Coordination Unit [...] and AIMA, I.P."

**Source:** Same as Proposition 5 ([Art. 27.º DL 237-A/2006](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=895A0027&nid=895&tabela=leis))

**Therefore:** The actual residency check follows this sequence:

1. **Intake (Art. 27.º n.º 2):** CRC checks form elements and documents. Residency duration is NOT checked here.
2. **Instruucao (Art. 27.º n.º 5):** CRC requests AIMA data. This is when residency duration is verified.
3. **Decision:** CRC assesses whether the applicant meets all Art. 6.º n.º 1 requirements -- at this point, as TC 1133/2025 confirmed.

By the time CRC reaches step 3, additional months of residence will have accrued. The procedural design of the law confirms the temporal framework: residency is a substantive requirement assessed at the decision stage, not a formal element checked at intake.

## IV. The New Law Does Not Apply to Applications Filed Before Its Promulgation

### Proposition 7: The new law has no legal force until promulgated and published

The new nationality law was voted by Parliament on 1 April 2026 (152 for, 64 against). It raises the residency requirement from 5 to 10 years (general) and 7 years (EU/CPLP nationals).

As of this writing, the law:

- Has **NOT** been promulgated by the President of the Republic
- Has **NOT** been published in the Diario da Republica
- Is **NOT** in force

Under Art. 5.º n.º 1 of the Codigo Civil:

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «A lei so se torna obrigatoria depois de publicada no jornal oficial.»

> **Translation:**
> "A law only becomes binding after publication in the official journal."

**Source:** [Art. 5.º Codigo Civil (pgdlisboa.pt)](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?nid=775&tabela=leis)

The current Art. 6.º n.º 1(b) still reads «cinco anos». Until the new law is promulgated and published, the 5-year requirement remains in force.

---

### Proposition 8: Applications filed under the current law are assessed under the current law's rules

TC 1133/2025 addressed precisely this scenario -- the temporal application of nationality law to pending applications:

> **Portuguese (verbatim, restated):**
> «O decreto pretendia fazer depender o deferimento de pedidos pendentes do preenchimento de requisitos na data de apresentacao, e nao na data da decisao, como ocorre no regime em vigor.»

> **Translation:**
> "The decree intended to make the approval of pending applications depend on the fulfillment of requirements at the date of submission, and not at the date of decision, as occurs in the regime in force."

The Court struck down Parliament's first attempt (October 2025) to change this framework. The current vote (1 April 2026) is a reapreciacao -- Parliament's second attempt, following the TC's ruling. But until it becomes law, the TC's characterization stands: the current regime assesses requirements at decision date.

**The filing date matters for one critical purpose:** it determines which law governs the application. An application filed while the current 5-year law is in force is assessed under that law's rules, including the decision-date assessment confirmed by TC 1133/2025. This is why the postal stamp date is the critical event -- it locks the application into the current regime.

---

### Proposition 9: Even if rejected, the applicant has 30 days to respond -- and the filing date is preserved

Art. 27.º n.º 3 of DL 237-A/2006:

> **Portuguese (verbatim):**
> «Se o conservador de registos ou o oficial de registos concluir que o requerimento deve ser liminarmente indeferido, o interessado e notificado dos fundamentos que conduzem ao indeferimento para que se pronuncie no prazo de 30 dias.»

> **Translation:**
> "If the registrar or registry official concludes that the application should be summarily rejected, the applicant is notified of the grounds for rejection so that they may respond within 30 days."

**Source:** Same as Proposition 5 ([Art. 27.º DL 237-A/2006](https://www.pgdlisboa.pt/leis/lei_mostra_articulado.php?artigo_id=895A0027&nid=895&tabela=leis))

**Therefore:** Even in a worst-case scenario, the filing date (postal stamp) is already registered. CRC received the application, and the date of receipt is on record. The applicant has 30 days to contest any rejection grounds. The filing date -- which determines which law applies -- survives any procedural exchange.

---

## V. Conclusion

The chain of reasoning:

1. **Art. 6.º n.º 1(b) Lei 37/81** requires five years of legal residence but does NOT anchor this requirement to the filing date. No «a data do pedido» or «imediatamente anteriores ao pedido» language.

2. **Art. 6.º n.º 8 Lei 37/81** uses explicit filing-date language («imediatamente anteriores ao pedido») for its own 5-year requirement -- proving the omission in Art. 6(1)(b) is deliberate.

3. **TC 1133/2025** (unanimous, 13 judges) confirmed that under the current regime, substantive requirements are assessed «na data da decisao do pedido» (at the date of decision on the application), not «na data da apresentacao do pedido» (at the date of submission). The Court struck down Parliament's attempt to change this as a violation of the «principio da protecao da confianca» (principle of protection of trust) under Art. 2.º CRP, because it would «afrontar as legitimas expectativas dos destinatarios com procedimentos pendentes» (affront the legitimate expectations of those with pending procedures). This ruling is binding and final.

4. **Art. 27.º n.º 2 DL 237-A/2006** defines the exclusive grounds for intake rejection: missing Art. 18.º n.º 4 elements or missing documents. Insufficient residency duration is NOT a rejection ground at intake.

5. **Art. 37.º n.º 7 + Art. 27.º n.º 5 DL 237-A/2006** confirm that AIMA residence data is obtained by CRC during instruucao (the investigation phase), after intake. The actual residency check happens at this stage, not at submission.

6. **Art. 5.º n.º 1 Codigo Civil** -- the new law has no force until published in the Diario da Republica. The current 5-year requirement remains in effect.

7. **TC 1133/2025** protects pending applications: they are assessed under the law in force at the time of filing, using the decision-date assessment framework.

8. **Art. 27.º n.º 3 DL 237-A/2006** guarantees 30 days' notice before any rejection. The filing date (postal stamp) is registered upon receipt and survives any procedural exchange.

**An application filed under the current 5-year law, before the new law is promulgated, remains pending. CRC must assess the residency requirement at the date of decision -- by which time the five years will have been reached. There is no lawful basis for rejecting the application at intake on the ground that five years have not yet elapsed.**
